Who We Are

A Community Devoted
to the Whole Person

St Philomena School follows a classical curriculum based on the philosophy of guiding students to a knowledge of the true, a love of what is truly good, and the contemplation of what is truly beautiful.

Our teacher-centred learning focuses on the Catholic Faith and the greatest thinkers, writers, artists, and events in history — an education of lasting worth. Our Day School brings this philosophy to life in the classroom, on the campus, and in the chapel.

Our Story
1999

The year St Philomena School was founded

For more than twenty-five years, St Philomena School has offered families a genuinely classical and faithful education. St Philomena School was established in response to the concerns of Catholic families about the available education choices for their children. Parents approached the Society of St Pius X to ask for assistance in developing a truly Catholic school.

Through the great generosity of Dr Felice and Mrs Marie Zaccari, eleven rural acres at 61 Koplick Road, Park Ridge, was purchased in February 1993. Under the intercession of St Philomena, all obstacles were finally overcome and the school opened on Tuesday 2 February 1999. Throughout, our mission has remained constant: to impart the rich and profound beauty of the traditional Catholic Faith, and to form young people of intellect, virtue, and wonder.

Principal's Welcome

A Message from
Fr Joseph Ockerse

St Philomena School, as an independent Catholic school of the Society of St Pius X, strives to deliver an authentically Catholic education.

It would be a mistake to suppose that a Catholic School is one with merely Catholic teachers, some regular instruction in Catechism, and the opportunity to receive the sacraments. A school can only be Catholic when it is directed by the Catholic philosophy of life.

St Philomena School exists to provide the pressing need of educating human beings to be authentically human — young men and women who know, love, and serve God, and who are thereby equipped to serve their neighbour and society.
