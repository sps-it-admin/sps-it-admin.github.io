The Trivium

Grammar, Logic
& Rhetoric

Our curriculum is built on the classical Trivium — the three arts of language: Grammar, Logic, and Rhetoric. These are not merely school subjects but the very tools by which a student learns to think, reason, and express truth clearly and beautifully.

Year 7 is the Grammar Phase, in which students lay the foundations of knowledge, master the forms of language, and cultivate the habits of careful, attentive study. Year 8 is the Logic Phase, in which students begin to question, analyse, and order what they have learned.

Year 7

The Grammar Phase

Laying the Foundations
01

Latin

Latin is central to the classical curriculum. Its study develops precision, memory, and an understanding of the roots of English and Western thought. Students begin with the fundamentals of grammar and translation.

02

Religion & Catechism

The Catholic Faith is not one subject among many — it is the light in which all subjects are understood. Students study the Baltimore Catechism and Sacred Scripture with care and devotion.

03

English Literature

Students read widely from the great works of the Western tradition — poetry, prose, and drama — developing their capacity to read attentively and write with clarity and precision.

04

History & Geography

Ancient and modern history situate students in the great story of Western civilisation. They learn not merely dates and events but causes, consequences, and the providential hand of God in history.

Year 8

The Logic Phase

Learning to Reason & Analyse
01

Formal Logic

In Year 8, students are introduced to formal Logic — the art of correct reasoning. They learn to identify valid and invalid arguments, recognise fallacies, and think with greater rigour and precision.

02

Rhetoric

Rhetoric is the art of speaking and writing persuasively and well. Students learn to order their thoughts, construct arguments, and communicate truth with confidence and grace.

03

Advanced Latin

Building on Year 7, students advance their Latin studies — reading adapted classical texts and deepening their understanding of the language that underlies the Western intellectual tradition.

04

Mathematics & Science

Mathematics and Science are taught with the same rigour and reverence for truth as every other subject. Students learn to observe the natural world with wonder and to reason about it with precision.

All Subjects
