Sport

Sport is a dynamic aspect of the co-curricular program at St Philomena School, in which self-control, respect, humility, teamwork and a healthy lifestyle are appreciated. St Philomena offers a range of sporting opportunities for students to foster their individual talents from Year 5-12 throughout the year.

Club Sport

St Philomena Sea Wolves are in our seventh year of competing in the South West Metro Basketball Friday night/Saturday morning league and have a team in each age group from U/12 to U/18, and for the Secondary Girls we participate in the Monday night Jimboomba Netball competition for U/18’s.

Interschool Sport

St Philomena competes in the local United Sports League outside school hours which encompasses students from Years 5 – 12 (Primary Thursday afternoons and Secondary Monday afternoons) across a variety of sports including Futsal, Basketball, Touch, Soccer and Netball.

Representative Sport

Students from 10-19 years can trial for Southern Scorpions district teams in a variety of sports every year. We are extremely proud of all the students who have been a part of local, state, and national competitions.

School Carnivals

During the year students also participate in the full school Cross Country, Athletics, and 10-19 Years swimming carnivals. Age champions and house champion’s titles are awarded, and students are then able to qualify for Southern Scorpions Carnivals. St Philomena currently have two sporting houses, Polding (Blue) and Chisholm (Green).

Sports Awards

At the conclusion of the sporting year, the annual sports awards ceremony is held. These awards acknowledge the achievements of all students across the school with various awards ranging from:

  • Age Champions
  • Players of the year for Basketball and Netball
  • State Representatives
